Study design

The study is a post-hoc analysis of CheckMate 040: a phase 1/2 multi-centric clinical trial that evaluated nivolumab in HCC patients. It had a dose-escalation and a dose-expansion phases, and about 85 participants in the Asian cohort and 182 in the general population group.

Patients over 18 years old who:
► have advanced HCC with or without viral hepatitis
► were previously treated with Sorafenib
► are not amenable to curative resection or local treatment.

Endpoints and Assessments

The primary endpoint was to assess the safety and tolerability of Nivolumab in both populations.
The secondary endpoint was to assess ORR, CR, DCR, DOR, and OS parameters in the trial.

Objective Response Rate (ORR)

The proportion of patients with tumor size reduction of a predefined amount and for a minimum time period [3]

Complete Response (CR)

Defined as no detectable evidence of tumor [3]

Complete Response (CR) Rate

The proportion of subjects whose best overall response (BOR) is CR in the population of interest [3]

Disease Control Rate (DCR)

The proportion of subjects whose best overall response is complete Response (CR), Partial response (PR) or stable disease (SD) in the population of interest [3]

Duration of Response (DOR)

The time between the date of first radiographic documented objective response and the date of the radiographic disease progression [3]

Overall Survival (OS)

The time from date of randomization to the date of death [3]

About Project Amber

Project Amber is a graduation project for Egypt Scholars 8th basic research Lab.
Amber is aimed at increasing the awareness about Hepatocellular carcinoma disease treatments by utilizing the data published from the CheckMate 040 clinical trial.
