Hi! Welcome to Project Amber.

We are going to walk you through a subgroup analysis of the CheckMate 040 clinical trial. You will learn about hepatocellular carcinoma, the available treatments for the disease, and more!

Before we do that, and in support of patients who suffer from liver cancer or any other type of malignancy, we included a button below that will direct you to the donation page of the Association of Friends of The National Cancer Free Initiative, where you can donate any amount of money and help save a life!

So, What is Hepatocellular Carcinoma?

Hepatocellular carcinoma is a fatal malignancy and the most common form of liver cancer; It is responsible for the death of 745,000 lives each year [1, 2]. In the following video from NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital, Dr. Halazun explains what is HCC and what are the disease's treatment options.

Heterogeneity of HCC

About Project Amber

Project Amber is a graduation project for Egypt Scholars 8th basic research Lab.
Amber is aimed at increasing the awareness about Hepatocellular carcinoma disease treatments by utilizing the data published from the CheckMate 040 clinical trial.
