Conclusion and References

References

Evidence Before This Study

■ Multikinase inhibitors such as sorafenib, lenvatinib, and regorafenib have been studied and shown survival benefits for HCC patients [4].

■ Tremelimumab: a CLTA-4 checkpoint inhibitor has also shown antitumor and antiviral activity in patients with advanced HCC and chronic hepatitis C [5].

■ Before this study, there was limited evidence suggesting that immune checkpoint inhibitors could be useful for these patients, but the potency of PD-1 inhibitors in melanoma provided a rationale for them to be used in the other malignancies.

Added Value of The Study

■ The study first reported PD-1 inhibitors in HCC patients.

■ Nivolumab has shown to be safe and effective in both Asian and western patients.

■ The drug is being evaluated as a first-line treatment in patients naive to sorafenib and as a second line for experienced patients.

About Project Amber

Project Amber is a graduation project for Egypt Scholars 8th basic research Lab.
Amber is aimed at increasing the awareness about Hepatocellular carcinoma disease treatments by utilizing the data published from the CheckMate 040 clinical trial.
